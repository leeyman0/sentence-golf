Sentence Golf!

Sentence golf is a lot like normal golf, except the players use words instead of clubs, and the holes to drive and putt are in the mind of the judges.

I got this idea from my reoccurring fascination of code golf and applying it to communication in general. A blog post is in the works for further discussion.

The Sport

Play of this sport needs four things:

  • A group of players to compete against one another
  • One or more impartial judges
  • The golf scoring program that can be found on this webpage below
  • A set of communication goals that the players will try to fulfill in the course of play and that the judges will evaluate

First, each player on their own will try to fill all communication goals within the amount of time allotted by the judges. The judges of that game control what resources the players use during the game. It is recommended that players have access to the golf scoring program to check their work.

The golf program grades each word of the text that the players use to achieve their communication goals by how frequent it is within the English language. The score of each word will range from 1 to 6. The more frequent the word is, the less it will score. If a word is not found in the dataset at all, a ten-point penalty plus a point for every letter in the word is incurred. The scores of all the words are combined to get the final score of the sentence or passage.

The judges evaluate how well each of the players did at each of the communication goals, considering factors such as clarity and how well the point is made. Using this evaluation and conferring amongst themselves, the judges will decide on each communication goal to give the player -10, 0, or +100 to the players' score.

After the judges add up all of the points, the player with the least points wins the competition.
